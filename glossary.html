Basic Financial Glossary

Asset
Something of value owned by an individual, company, or organization, such as cash, investments, or property.
Budget
A plan that outlines projected income and expenses over a specified period, often used for personal or business financial management.
Debt
An amount of money owed by an individual, company, or organization to a lender or creditor.
Interest
A fee paid for borrowing money or the return earned on an investment.
Investment
An asset or item acquired with the expectation that it will generate income or appreciate in value over time.
Portfolio
A collection of investments held by an individual, company, or organization.
Revenue
The income generated from the sale of goods or services.
Stock
A type of security that represents ownership in a company and provides the holder with certain rights, such as voting rights and a share in the company's profits.
Tax
A compulsory financial charge imposed by the government on individuals and businesses to fund public expenditures.
Withdrawal
The act of taking money out of an account or investment.
